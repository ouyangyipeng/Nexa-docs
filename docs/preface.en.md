Before diving into Nexa's core syntax, let's take a step back and think: What structural challenges does the industry currently face when developing agent systems centered on Large Language Models (LLMs)? How does Nexa, based on compiler theory and language design, stand out and achieve a dimensional reduction from "application-layer frameworks" to "low-level primitives"?

- 📖 **[Background: From Language Models to Agent-Native Computing Paradigm](preface_background.md)**: A comprehensive review of the evolution of large language models and Agents, an in-depth analysis of the underlying engineering pain points faced by Dify/Coze (low-code paradigm) and Python/LangChain (general-purpose programming language paradigm), and an explanation of how Nexa reshapes native primitives as the "third paradigm."
- ⚔️ **[Typical Scenarios: In-depth Comparison of Nexa and Existing Orchestration Methods](preface_scenarios.md)**: A practical comparison of Nexa's dimensional advantages over other frameworks through three typical scenarios: complex code generation, long-cycle financial report generation, and high-risk IT operations requiring "Human-in-the-Loop" (HITL).
